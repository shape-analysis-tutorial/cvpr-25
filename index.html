3D Shape Analysis: From Classical Optimization to Learning-Based Matching

The field of 3D shape analysis is concerned with the extraction of “useful” information from geometric data. Shape analysis has a high relevance for a wide range of applications, such as autonomous driving, biomedicine, or augmented/virtual reality. A core task of 3D shape analysis is shape matching, i.e. identifying correspondences between given shapes. While traditional shape matching methods rely on optimizing a task-specific objective function, modern shape matching oftentimes involves data-driven components. We will first introduce traditional methods for shape matching, starting with the linear and quadratic assignment problems. We then present product graph formalism in different settings, including 2D to 2D, 2D to 3D or shape to image, and 3D to 3D shape matching. Furthermore, we then discuss problem formulations such as shape interpolation, as well as learning-based shape correspondence methods, including spectral approaches that provide efficient structure and circumvent the need for annotations altogether. Additionally, we discuss the practical relevance of these methods to application domains in image-to-image and shape-to-image correspondence such as medical imaging, and address the role of recent developments in foundation models for shape analysis. Finally, the tutorial will conclude by addressing the ongoing challenges of shape matching, including handling partial shapes, and will explore potential future directions in the field.
